Beef Street Taco

The History of Tacos

The origin of tacos are unknown in actuality. However, there are theories that they date from the 18th century and in the silver mines in Mexico since in those mines the word "taco: referred to the little charges they would use to excavate the ore. The first references to tacos in any sort of archive or dictionary come from the end of the 19th century. And one of the first types of tacos described is called tacos de minero: miner's taco. In conclusion, although there is no clear origin of the taco, it is considered a traditional mexican dish.

Recipe for Beef Tacos

You will need*:

  • olive oil
  • skirt streak
  • yellow onion
  • ground chipotle powder
  • chili powder
  • cumin (toasted)
  • kosher salt
  • white corn tortillas
  • white onion
  • cilantro
  • hot sauce and/or salsa
  • diced tomato (optional)

*you can add and take away some ingredients (which are merely toppings) depending on what you would like as toppings

  1. You will start by finely mincing your beef (1-1/4 pounds) with a sharp knife
  2. Then you will drizzle 2 teaspoons olive oil into a deep sided skillet on medium high heat
  3. Once hot, add in your beef and 1 finely diced yellow onion
  4. Stir and cook until almost all traces of pink are gone (in the beef)
  5. Measure and add in a 3/4 teaspoon of ground chipotle powder, 1/2 teaspoon chili powder, 1/2 teaspoon cumin, and 3/4 teaspoon kosher salt or to taste (add as much as you see fit)
  6. Stir and cook until the steak is not longer pink
  7. While the meat is cooking, quickly toast one side of the tortillas in a dry skillet over medium-high heat
  8. Lastly, put them both together: fill your tortillas with spoonfuls of the meat mixture and then top it with lots of onions, cilantro, and whatever else you'd like (cheese, sourcream, lime, salsa, etc)
